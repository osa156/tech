How to Make Perfect Amala and Ewedu Soup

By Chef Adeola February 8, 2026 60 minutes Medium

Amala and Ewedu Soup is a classic Yoruba delicacy that has won the hearts of Nigerians across all ethnic groups. The smooth, dark brown amala made from yam flour pairs perfectly with the viscous, green ewedu soup for a truly satisfying meal.

What is Amala and Ewedu Soup?

Amala (also known as Amala Isu) is a smooth, brown swallow made from dried yam flour (elubo). It has a distinctive earthy flavor and smooth texture that makes it a favorite in Yoruba households. Ewedu Soup (also called Jute or Molokhia leaves) is a mucilaginous soup made from ewedu leaves, locust beans (iru), and crayfish. When combined, they create a meal that is both comforting and highly nutritious.

Smooth Amala Texture

Ingredients You'll Need

For the Amala:

Yam Flour (Elubo)

2 cups

Boiling Water

4-5 cups

For the Ewedu Soup:

Fresh Ewedu Leaves

1 large bunch

Locust Beans (Iru)

1 tablespoon

Ground Crayfish

2 tablespoons

Palm Oil

2 tablespoons

Assorted Meat

300g, precooked

Seasoning Cubes

2 cubes

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare the Ewedu Leaves

Pick the ewedu leaves from the stalks carefully. Wash thoroughly in clean water to remove all dirt and sand. This step is crucial as ewedu leaves can hold a lot of grit.

2

Cook the Ewedu

Bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a pot. Add the washed ewedu leaves and boil for 5-8 minutes until tender. The leaves will reduce significantly in volume.

3

Blend the Ewedu

Using a blender or traditional ewedu broom (ijabe), blend the cooked leaves until smooth and viscous. If using a blender, pulse briefly to maintain some texture. Add a little cooking water if too thick.

4

Season the Soup

Return the blended ewedu to low heat. Add the locust beans (iru), ground crayfish, seasoning cubes, and salt to taste. Stir well and simmer for 3 minutes. Add palm oil and stir.

5

Add Protein

Add your precooked assorted meat (beef, goat meat, or smoked fish). Stir gently and simmer for another 2 minutes. Remove from heat immediately to preserve the green color.

6

Make the Amala

Bring 4 cups of water to a rolling boil in a sturdy pot. Reduce heat to medium. Gradually sprinkle the yam flour while stirring continuously with a wooden paddle (omorogun) to prevent lumps.

7

Knead to Perfection

Keep stirring and kneading the mixture against the sides of the pot. Add small amounts of hot water if too thick. The amala should be smooth, soft, and slightly elastic with no lumps.

8

Wrap and Serve

Wet a small bowl or wrap with nylon. Scoop amala into it and turn upside down onto a plate to create a smooth dome shape. Serve hot with the ewedu soup.

Pro Tips for Success

  • Always use freshly harvested ewedu leaves for the best viscosity and flavor
  • Add a pinch of kaun (potash) if your ewedu isn't drawing enough
  • The water for amala must be at a rolling boil - this ensures proper cooking
  • Stir amala vigorously to achieve a smooth, lump-free consistency
  • Don't overcook ewedu or it will turn brown and lose nutrients
  • Serve amala immediately - it hardens quickly as it cools
  • For extra flavor, add a little blended okra to the ewedu

Nutritional Information

This meal provides a balanced combination of carbohydrates, proteins, and essential vitamins:

Nutrient Per Serving % Daily Value
Calories 380-450 kcal 19-22%
Carbohydrates 75g 25%
Protein 18g 36%
Fiber 12g 48%
Iron 5.2mg 29%
Calcium 180mg 18%

Serving Suggestions

Amala and Ewedu is traditionally eaten with your fingers (right hand only). Take a small portion of amala, dip it in the ewedu soup, and swallow. The soup is often accompanied by a side of buka stew (obe ata) with assorted meat for a complete "Abula" experience.

For the authentic Buka experience, serve with: - Ewedu soup (as prepared) - Buka stew (spicy tomato-based stew) - Assorted meat (beef, goat meat, shaki, ponmo) - Fresh fish or smoked fish

How to Make Perfect Eba and Okro Soup

By Chef Adaobi January 23, 2026 45 minutes Medium

Eba and Okro Soup is a beloved Nigerian delicacy that combines the starchy comfort of garri with the nutritious, slippery texture of okro. This dish is a staple in many Nigerian homes, especially in the southwestern region.

What is Eba and Okro Soup?

Eba (also known as Garri) is a staple swallow made from dried grated cassava (garri) mixed with hot water to form a smooth, dough-like consistency. Okro Soup (Ila in Yoruba) is a thick, viscous soup made from okra vegetables, palm oil, and various proteins. When combined, they create a meal that is both satisfying and nutritionally balanced.

Perfect Eba Texture

Ingredients You'll Need

For the Eba:

Garri (White or Yellow)

2 cups

Boiling Water

3-4 cups

For the Okro Soup:

Fresh Okro

20-25 pieces, chopped

Palm Oil

1/2 cup

Ground Crayfish

3 tablespoons

Locust Beans (Iru)

1 tablespoon

Goat Meat/Beef

500g, precooked

Seasoning Cubes

2-3 cubes

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare the Meat

Wash and season your meat (goat meat, beef, or assorted meat) with seasoning cubes, salt, and onions. Cook until tender and set aside. Reserve the meat stock for the soup.

2

Prepare the Okro

Wash the okro thoroughly and chop finely. For extra "draw" (sliminess), you can grate half of the okro and chop the other half. This creates a perfect texture balance.

3

Heat the Palm Oil

In a pot, heat the palm oil on medium heat. Do not bleach the oil - just warm it enough to release its aroma. Add the chopped onions and saut until translucent.

4

Add Flavor Base

Add the locust beans (iru), ground crayfish, and pepper blend. Stir fry for 2-3 minutes until fragrant. This creates the flavor foundation for your soup.

5

Add Meat and Stock

Add the precooked meat and meat stock. Bring to a gentle boil. The stock adds depth of flavor that water alone cannot provide.

6

Add the Okro

Add the chopped okro and stir immediately. Cook for 5-7 minutes on medium heat. Do not overcook - the okro should remain bright green and retain some texture.

7

Season and Simmer

Add seasoning cubes and salt to taste. If using spinach or ugwu leaves, add them now and simmer for 2 more minutes. Remove from heat.

8

Make the Eba

Boil water in a pot. Once boiling, reduce heat and gradually sprinkle garri while stirring continuously with a wooden spatula. Stir until smooth and firm. Mold into portions.

Pro Tips for Success

  • For extra draw in your okro soup, add a pinch of baking soda or kaun (potash)
  • Always use freshly harvested okro for the best texture and flavor
  • The water for eba must be boiling hot - lukewarm water will result in lumpy eba
  • Stir eba vigorously to achieve a smooth, lump-free consistency
  • Add smoked fish or stockfish for extra depth of flavor
  • Serve immediately while hot - eba hardens as it cools

Nutritional Information

This meal provides a balanced combination of carbohydrates, proteins, and essential vitamins:

Nutrient Per Serving % Daily Value
Calories 450-550 kcal 22-28%
Carbohydrates 65g 22%
Protein 25g 50%
Fiber 8g 32%
Vitamin C 35mg 58%
Iron 4.5mg 25%

Serving Suggestions

Eba and Okro Soup is traditionally eaten with your fingers (the right hand only). Take a small portion of eba, dip it in the soup, and swallow without chewing. This might take practice, but it's the authentic way to enjoy this dish!

For a complete meal, serve with a chilled glass of palm wine, zobo drink, or chilled water. Some people also enjoy adding a side of fresh pepper sauce (ata din din) for extra heat.
