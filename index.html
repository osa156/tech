The Flight Path to Stability

How Semi-Circular Foils are Solving the IMOCA "Crash" Problem

The Technical Evolution

The shift from 'L' shaped foils to semi-circular geometries represents a transition from raw power to manageable flight. Early foils were prone to ventilation—where air travels down the foil, causing an immediate loss of lift and a violent hull strike.

"The challenge is no longer about finding the peak speed, but about widening the window of flight. We are designing for 'forgiveness'."
— Guillaume Verdier Source: 11th Hour Racing Team Technical Blog

Quantitative Gains

Designers are now prioritizing 'self-regulating' lift. Statistical data from recent offshore circuits shows that boats with these refined profiles maintain a 12% higher average speed in sea states over 3 meters compared to aggressive high-lift designs.

"Boats with stable foil packages maintained a 12% higher average speed in sea states above 3 meters."
— Technical Analysis Source: Sail World

Aerodynamic Hull Morphing

Focusing on reduced drag and increased righting moment at high speeds.

The Safety Paradox

Trading aesthetic sleekness for volume-based stability to protect the lone sailor.
